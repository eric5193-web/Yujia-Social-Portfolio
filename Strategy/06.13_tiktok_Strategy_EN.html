Core Finding

Follower count doubled in 8 days, but growth is heavily concentrated in the first 2 posts — responsible for 206 new followers, or 74% of total 8-day growth. This is not luck. It's the direct result of algorithmic distribution: the first two posts achieved 18–22% cover CTR and entered Douyin's recommendation feed, reaching cold audiences. The other three posts had 0% cover CTR and only reached existing followers. The bottleneck isn't content quality — it's whether the cover can pass the algorithm's discovery threshold.

⚠ Urgent Signal

Unfollow rates across all posts are extremely low (0.02–0.17%), which confirms content quality is not the problem — people who watch genuinely like what they see. The issue is upstream: the cover image determines whether the algorithm distributes the post to new users. When cover CTR = 0%, Douyin treats the post as irrelevant to cold audiences and stops pushing it.

Save Rate Finding

Jun 13 "Forever is too far" has a 0.99% save rate — 2–5× higher than any other post. Only 8 hours old with 1,005 views, yet already 10 saves. A save tells the algorithm this content has lasting emotional value worth returning to. If this post gains algorithmic distribution, its save rate will aggressively accelerate further push. This is the strongest content quality signal in the account right now.

Comment Rate Finding

Comment rate follows a clear inverse pattern: more reach = lower comment rate. Jun 05 reached 11,000 with 0.16% comment rate; Jun 12 reached 3,109 but hit 0.35%. The reason: algorithmic audiences are strangers who like but rarely comment; existing followers are the ones who want to talk. High comment rate = fan loyalty. Low comment rate = algorithmic reach. Both are good — just different signals.

Correlation 01 · Cover CTR → Views (Decisive Positive)

Cover CTR is the direct determinant of view volume. 18–22% CTR = 9–11K views; 0% CTR = 1–3K views — a gap of 4–11×. Every other metric difference between the posts is an amplification of this first-step gap. Fix the cover, fix everything downstream.

Correlation 02 · Views ↑ → Comment Rate ↓ (Clear Inverse)

The wider the reach, the lower the comment rate. Jun 05 (11K views) had 0.16% comment rate; Jun 12 (3K views) hit 0.35%. Algorithmic audiences like but don't comment; existing fans are the most active commenters. This is a healthy distribution pattern, not a problem to solve.

Correlation 03 · Like Rate ≠ Follow Rate (Counterintuitive Weak Link)

Post 3 has a 7.44% like rate (one of the highest) but the lowest follow rate at 0.88%. Post 4 has a 7.14% like rate but the highest follow rate at 1.64%. A like means "this is good." A follow means "I want more of this person." They require different content depth. High like rate does not mean the account is worth following.

Correlation 04 · Save Rate → Breakout Potential (Strongest Predictor)

Post 5 simultaneously holds the highest like rate (13.23%), highest save rate (0.99%), lowest unfollow count (0), and zero "not interested" signals — four quality metrics resonating together. This is the strongest latent breakout signal in the account. The only missing piece is distribution. A cover fix could make this the next viral post.

Final Strategic Synthesis
The Algorithm Truth

Douyin asks two questions: Is the cover compelling enough to click? and Does the content hold attention once clicked? All 5 posts score similarly on retention (47–55% skip rate) — content is fine. The only variable is the cover.

The Content Truth

There is no bad content here. There is only "good content that couldn't pass the cover gate" and "good content that could." Post 3's like rate (7.44%) is higher than the breakout Post 1 (6.98%) — it just never got the chance to reach more people.

The Next Step Truth

Post 5 is the most urgent priority: 13% like rate, 1% save rate, 0 unfollows. Swap the cover for a high-contrast night version and add a "save this frame" CTA. This post has the highest probability of becoming the next breakout in the account.

What if Post 4 had entered the recommendation feed?

Post 4 has the highest follow rate in the account at 1.64%. If it had received the same reach as Post 1 (11,000 views), at 1.64% it would have generated ~180 new followers — 57% more than Post 1's 115. This confirms the core finding: the creative capability is already there. What's missing is the ability to make the algorithm see it — and that solution is one thing: fix the cover.
