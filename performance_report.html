Section 3 · Algorithm Differences
East Meets Feed
Platform Mechanics
How Each Algorithm Works — and Why It Mattered
Understanding the distribution logic behind each platform explains why the same content produced radically different results
Instagram
Social Graph First
Core Logic
Instagram's algorithm prioritizes content from accounts users already follow. Discovery (Explore, Reels) is secondary and requires strong early engagement signals to break into non-followers' feeds.
Key Ranking Signals
Saves & shares weighted higher than likes — indicate content utility
Comments & replies signal active community engagement
Time-spent on post (carousel swipes, video watch %) matters
Profile visits after seeing content boost reach
Existing follower relationship = higher distribution priority
Impact on This Campaign
With 2,387 existing followers, Instagram had a built-in audience. The Met post generated 141 profile visits — strong conversion signal. But follower growth was minimal because Instagram's Explore reach requires either paid boost or viral-level save rate.
Douyin
Interest Graph · ForYou
Core Logic
Douyin uses a pure interest graph — follower count is nearly irrelevant. Every post enters a small test pool. If early engagement exceeds a threshold, it cascades to exponentially larger audiences. Any account can go viral on the first post.
Key Ranking Signals
Completion rate (watch full video) is the #1 signal
Early engagement velocity (first 30–60 min) determines cascade
Shares = high-trust signal, triggers wider distribution
Comment sentiment analyzed — emotional resonance amplified
流量激励 system rewards quality content with paid traffic boosts
Impact on This Campaign
Starting from 13 followers, Post 1 triggered the cascade: 24K plays, 307 traffic incentive points, and +155 followers in 48 hours. This is only possible on Douyin — the algorithm treated a new account identically to an established one.
Xiaohongshu
SEO + Community Signals
Core Logic
Xiaohongshu functions like a search engine + social feed hybrid. Topic tags (#tags) are the primary discovery mechanism. Without tags, content reaches only existing followers unless the cover image and title generate organic click-throughs.
Key Ranking Signals
Tag matching — posts appear in tag search results (critical)
Cover image click-through rate (CTR) drives initial distribution
Saves weighted highly — signals "want to revisit" utility
Location tags increase geo-based discovery
Keyword density in title and caption affects search ranking
Impact on This Campaign
Post 1 (The Met) achieved 23.9% cover CTR and a 6.2% interaction rate (rated "Good" by the platform). But without topic tags, Posts 2 and 3 had no discovery pathway — averaging only 172 views, relying entirely on the 530-person follower base.

Strategic Conclusion
The three platforms operate on fundamentally different distribution philosophies. Douyin rewards emotion and content quality regardless of account size — making it the highest-leverage platform for a new creator. Instagram rewards an existing audience and is best suited for deepening relationships with followers already acquired. Xiaohongshu rewards search intent and tag strategy — it functions more like a discovery engine than a social network, and will underperform without an SEO-style tagging approach. A winning cross-platform strategy treats each platform as a distinct product, not a distribution channel.
